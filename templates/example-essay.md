# Example Essay: A Template

An essay in this workflow is a short markdown file —
typically 400 to 1200 words — written when an idea needs
more room than a chat turn and less structure than a spec.

## Shape

- Start with a title (`# Title`) and a brief framing
  paragraph. The framing should tell the reader what the
  essay is about, not summarize what it concludes. Reveal
  shouldn't happen at the top.
- Use `##` subheadings for each major move. Keep them few.
  Three or four is plenty.
- Prefer paragraphs over bullets for anything that has a
  reasoning chain. Bullets are for enumerations.
- End cleanly. Essays don't need to "wrap up" — they can
  stop when the thought is done.

## Writing posture

An essay is a place to think out loud, not to perform
completeness. You're allowed to be wrong in a paragraph and
right by the next one. The reader can comment on any
paragraph they disagree with, so you don't have to
anticipate every objection up front.

## Durability

Most essays are disposable. They're written to help a
thought take shape; once the thought is compressed into
memory or a decision, the essay can go. A few turn out to
be durable — read many times, referenced from sidecars or
memory — and earn a permanent home.

Write freely. Don't over-edit. If the essay turns out to
matter, it can be polished later; if it doesn't, the
editing effort was wasted.

## How to use this template

Copy this file, rename it, replace the body with your own
essay, and (optionally) create a sibling
`your-essay.md.comments.json` seeded with empty comments or
a few examples to show the inline-comment mechanic.
